Naruto is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Masashi Kishimoto. It tells the story of Naruto Uzumaki, a young ninja who seeks recognition from his peers and dreams of becoming the Hokage, the leader of his village. The story is told in two parts: the first set in Naruto's pre-teen years, and the second in his teens. The series is based on two one-shot manga by Kishimoto: Karakuri, which earned Kishimoto an honorable mention in Shueisha's monthly Hop Step Award the following year, and Naruto.

Naruto was serialized in Shueisha's magazine Weekly Shonen Jump from September 1999 to November 2014, and released in tankobon form in 72 volumes. The manga was adapted into an anime television series produced by Pierrot and Aniplex, which broadcast 220 episodes in Japan from 2002 to 2007; the English dub of the series aired on Cartoon Network and YTV from 2005 to 2009. Naruto: Shippuden, a sequel to the original series, premiered in Japan in 2007, and ended in 2017, after 500 episodes. The English dub was broadcast on Disney XD from 2009 to 2011, before being moved exclusively to Adult Swim's Toonami programming block in January 2014.

In the village of Konohagakure, the Hidden Leaf Village, lives Naruto Uzumaki, a hyperactive and clumsy adolescent ninja who constantly searches for approval and recognition, as well as to become Hokage, who is acknowledged as the leader and strongest of all the village's ninja. The story begins with the Nine-Tailed Demon Fox, Kurama, a powerful tailed beast that attacked Konohagakure twelve years before, killing many of its people, before being sealed away in the body of newborn Naruto by his father, the Fourth Hokage Minato Namikaze, at the cost of his life.

Konoha grew to revile Naruto, perceiving him as the Demon Fox itself. Although the Third Hokage outlawed speaking about anything related to the Demon Fox, the children, taking their cues from their parents, inherit the same animosity toward Naruto. In his thirst to be acknowledged, Naruto vows he will one day become the greatest Hokage Konoha has ever known. Naruto becomes a ninja, joining Team 7 with Sasuke Uchiha, Sakura Haruno, and their teacher Kakashi Hatake. They embark on missions throughout the Land of Fire and beyond, encountering rival ninjas, ancient mysteries, and the looming threat of an organization called Akatsuki.

The series explores themes of perseverance, friendship, the cost of war, and the burden carried by those born into circumstance. Naruto himself embodies the belief that hard work matters more than innate talent, that being acknowledged for who you are matters more than where you come from, and that the loneliness of being different can be transformed into the strength to protect others. His ninja way, drilled into him by everyone he meets and refined into his own creed, is simple: never go back on your word.

The world of Naruto is built on the elemental nations, hidden villages of ninja with shared traditions and conflicting interests. Each village trains its young into shinobi who serve as soldiers, spies, and assassins for hire. The five great villages are Konohagakure in the Land of Fire, Sunagakure in the Land of Wind, Kirigakure in the Land of Water, Iwagakure in the Land of Earth, and Kumogakure in the Land of Lightning. Each village is led by a Kage, a leader chosen for power and wisdom. The tailed beasts, including the Nine-Tails sealed inside Naruto, are creatures of immense chakra distributed across these villages as living weapons.

Among the recurring techniques of Naruto's arsenal, the Rasengan stands as one of the most iconic. Created by his father, the Fourth Hokage, the Rasengan is a swirling sphere of pure chakra held in the user's palm, requiring no hand seals and capable of devastating short-range damage. Naruto inherits the technique through the teaching of Jiraiya, his godfather and one of the legendary Sannin, and refines it across the series into variants like the Rasenshuriken. The Rasengan represents not only raw power but the lineage Naruto carries unknowingly through most of the first series — the legacy of a father he never knew.

The story of Naruto Uzumaki is, at its core, the story of a boy who refused to be defined by what was sealed inside him, and chose instead to be defined by what he chose to do with it.
